My research focuses on Computational Creativity — building computational models that capture and assess creativity through conceptual abstraction.

I am Michael Evan Santoso, a PhD student at Ritsumeikan University in Japan, in the College of Information Science and Engineering, where I work in the E-Society Laboratory led by Prof. Victor V. Kryssanov. I am originally from Indonesia.

My research centers on Computational Creativity: modeling and assessing creativity through conceptual abstraction. This builds on my background in Natural Language Processing and topic modeling — including generative topic modeling, transformer-based text segmentation, abstractive summarization, few-shot learning, and character recognition. I completed my Bachelor's (2019–2023) and Master's (2023–2025) degrees at Ritsumeikan University, and am now pursuing my PhD there.

You can read more about my research, browse my publications, or view my CV.
