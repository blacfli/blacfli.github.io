Research

My work explores how computational systems can model, generate, and evaluate creativity, grounding Computational Creativity in mathematical frameworks from NLP, topic modeling, and information theory.

Computational Creativity via Conceptual Abstraction

I investigate how creativity can be modeled and assessed computationally, with a focus on conceptual abstraction as a mechanism for generating and evaluating novel ideas. The goal is to make creativity measurable and reproducible rather than purely subjective, connecting cognitive notions of creativity to concrete computational representations.

Generative Topic Modeling & Information-Theoretic Foundations

I study topic models as mathematical frameworks for organizing and generating text, including data-selection strategies grounded in information theory. This treats topic modeling not only as a tool but as a lens for understanding structure and content in language, where information-theoretic criteria guide which data is most useful for generative modeling.

NLP for Segmentation, Summarization & Recognition

I work on transformer-based text segmentation and abstractive summarization, including benchmarking for Indonesian text, and I am interested in few-shot learning and character recognition for low-resource and historical documents. These efforts share a focus on robust language and vision models under limited data.
